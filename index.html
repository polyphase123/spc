Impact on Pricing

The CPT (₱12,413) is higher than the average price because it accounts for these "Spike" intervals where peaking plants (often Oil/LNG) set the price. The 3-day rolling window is designed to capture sustained high prices during the high-demand days (Tuesday-Wednesday).

Consumer & Utility Impact

What these changes mean for Distribution Utilities (DUs) and End-Users.

Distribution Utilities

  • Higher Exposure Threshold: DUs exposed to the spot market will see prices rise higher (up to ₱12,413 avg) before the cap protection kicks in, compared to the previous ₱9,000 CPT.

  • Supply Security: The new rates ensure that peaking plants (Peakers) recover their fuel costs, reducing the risk of plants shutting down during critical demand, thereby stabilizing supply for DUs.

Residential Consumers

  • Generation Charge: During peak summer months, the Generation Charge component of the bill may see a slight increase since the capped price (SPC) is now 19% higher (₱7,423 vs ₱6,245).

  • Protection Mechanism: Despite the increase, the mechanism prevents runaway prices (e.g., ₱32,000/MWh) from sustaining for long periods, protecting consumers from extreme bill shocks.

Key Takeaway: While the cap has increased to reflect real fuel costs (Coal/LNG), it remains a vital safeguard to limit extreme volatility while ensuring power plants remain operational.

Annex A: Implementing Rules

Essential guidelines from the "Rules on the Imposition of Secondary Price Cap".

Only Oil-based and LNG (Liquified Natural Gas) power plants are eligible.

They can recover the unrecovered portion of their verified fuel and Variable Operating and Maintenance (VOM) costs if the SPC is insufficient to cover these costs. Other technologies are not eligible.

The Secondary Price Cap remains in force until the rolling Generator Weighted Average Price (GWAP), measured over the 72-hour period, falls below the Cumulative Price Threshold (CPT) of ₱12,413/MWh.
The regional/island SPC applies when a grid interconnection (e.g., HVDC) is on outage. It uses the same parameters (SPC value, CPT, rolling period) as the system-wide SPC but applies specifically to the affected region's GWAP.
